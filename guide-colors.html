Guide: Colors & EMS

Maine Coons use EMS coding: breed + color letters + pattern/white numbers. This page explains how to decode those labels quickly.

EMS Letter Codes (Color)

CodeMeaningNotes
nBlackBase black series
aBlueDilute black
dRedBase red series
eCreamDilute red
fBlack tortieBlack + red
gBlue tortieDilute tortie
sSilver (inhibitor)Creates silver/smoke effects
wWhiteNo visible pigment
yGoldenWarm undercoat expression

EMS Number Codes

CodeMeaningUse case
01VanMostly white, very limited color
02HarlequinMostly white with several colored areas
03BicolorSubstantial white + color distribution
09Unspecified white spottingSmaller white areas
11ShadedAbout 1/4 hair tipping colored
12ShellAbout 1/8 hair tipping colored
21Agouti (unspecified tabby)Pattern present but not clear enough to classify
22Classic tabbyMarble/swirled pattern
23Mackerel tabbyTiger-like stripes
24Spotted tabbyBroken stripes into spots
25Ticked tabbyBanded hairs with softer body pattern

Smoke, Shaded, Shell

TypeTipping amountCommon EMSLook
SmokeAbout 1/2 coloredns, ds, fsDarker coat with pale undercoat visible in movement
ShadedAbout 1/4 coloredns 11, as 11, ds 11, es 11, fs 11, gs 11Lighter, dimensional appearance
ShellAbout 1/8 coloredns 12, as 12, ds 12, es 12, fs 12, gs 12Very pale tipping, rarest expression

Fast Reading Examples

  • MCO n = black solid.
  • MCO ns = black smoke.
  • MCO n 22 = black classic tabby.
  • MCO ns 22 = black silver classic tabby.
  • MCO fs 03 = smoky tortie with bicolor white.
